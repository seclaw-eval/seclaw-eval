Global Carbon Emissions and Their Impact on Climate Change

Dr. Sarah Chen, Dr. Raj Patel, Dr. Maria Okonkwo, Dr. James Whitfield, Dr. Yuki Tanaka

Abstract: This study presents a comprehensive analysis of the relationship between anthropogenic carbon emissions and global climate change. Using data from 150 monitoring stations across six continents over a 30-year period, we demonstrate a statistically significant correlation (p < 0.001) between rising CO2 concentrations and increasing global mean surface temperatures. Our findings indicate that carbon emissions from industrial activities are the primary driver of observed warming trends, accounting for approximately 78% of the temperature increase since 1970. Regional analysis reveals that areas with the highest per-capita emissions show the most pronounced warming effects. We also examine feedback mechanisms, including permafrost thawing and ocean acidification, which amplify the initial warming signal. The results strongly support the consensus that reducing carbon emissions is essential to mitigating further climate destabilization. Policy implications and recommendations for emission reduction targets are discussed.
