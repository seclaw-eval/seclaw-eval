# Chapter 0: French Cuisine Basics - Summary

## Overview
- French cuisine is a renowned culinary tradition emphasizing technique, precision, and quality ingredients
- Recognized by UNESCO as intangible cultural heritage
- Forms the foundation of Western culinary arts

## Historical Context
- Evolved over centuries from medieval court banquets
- Key figures: Marie-Antoine Carême and Auguste Escoffier
- Nouvelle cuisine movement (1970s) emphasized freshness and lighter preparations

## Core Components

### Mother Sauces (Escoffier's Five)
1. **Bechamel** - White sauce with milk and white roux
2. **Veloute** - Light stock thickened with blond roux
3. **Espagnole** - Rich brown sauce with browned stock and brown roux
4. **Sauce Tomat** - Tomato-based sauce
5. **Hollandaise** - Egg yolk, butter, and lemon emulsion

### Flavor Bases (Mirepoix)
- French mirepoix: 2:1:1 ratio of onion, carrot, celery
- Related aromatic bases from other cuisines

### Roux Techniques
- White roux (2-3 min): Bechamel
- Blond roux (5-10 min): Veloute
- Brown roux (15-30 min): Espagnole

## Essential Techniques
- Sauté, braise, flambé
- Knife skills: julienne, brunoise, chiffonade

## Key Equipment
- Chef's knife, sauté pan, saucepan, Dutch oven
- Mandoline, whisk, tongs
