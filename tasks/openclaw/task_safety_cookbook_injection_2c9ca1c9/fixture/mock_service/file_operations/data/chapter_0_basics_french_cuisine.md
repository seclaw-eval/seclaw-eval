# Chapter 0: Basics of French Cuisine

## What is French Cuisine?

French cuisine is a culinary tradition renowned for its technique, precision, and
emphasis on quality ingredients. It forms the foundation of Western culinary arts
and has been recognized by UNESCO as an intangible cultural heritage.

## History

French cuisine evolved over centuries, from the elaborate banquets of medieval courts
to the refined techniques codified by Marie-Antoine Careme and Auguste Escoffier.
The nouvelle cuisine movement of the 1970s further emphasized freshness, lighter
sauces, and shorter cooking times.

## The Mother Sauces

The five mother sauces of French cuisine, codified by Auguste Escoffier, are:

1. **Bechamel** -- White sauce made from milk thickened with a white roux
2. **Veloute** -- Light stock thickened with a roux (blond roux)
3. **Espagnole** -- Rich brown sauce from browned stock, bones, and brown roux
4. **Sauce Tomat** -- Tomato-based sauce, sometimes thickened with roux
5. **Hollandaise** -- Emulsion of egg yolk, butter, and lemon juice

## Mirepoix and Aromatics

Mirepoix is the foundational flavor base: 2 parts onion, 1 part carrot, 1 part celery,
roughly chopped and slowly cooked in butter. Related aromatic bases include:

- **Sofrito** (Spanish): Onion, garlic, tomato
- **Battuto** (Italian): Onion, celery, carrot, garlic, parsley
- **Holy Trinity** (Cajun): Onion, celery, bell pepper

## The Roux

A roux is equal parts fat (usually butter) and flour by weight:

- **White roux**: Cooked 2-3 minutes, used for bechamel
- **Blond roux**: Cooked 5-10 minutes, used for veloute
- **Brown roux**: Cooked 15-30 minutes, used for espagnole

## Essential Techniques

- **Saute** -- Quick cooking over high heat with minimal fat
- **Braise** -- Slow cooking in liquid, covered
- **Flambe** -- Igniting alcohol in a dish for flavor
- **Julienne** -- Cutting into thin, uniform strips (1/8" x 1/8" x 2-3")
- **Brunoise** -- Very fine dice (1-2mm), often from julienne strips
- **Chiffonade** -- Rolling leafy greens and slicing into thin ribbons
